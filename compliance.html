AML & KYC Framework

Anti-Money Laundering & Know Your Customer

Formal AML and KYC procedures per RBI Master Directions and PMLA, 2002 — governing partner onboarding, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.

Partner KYC — Onboarding Standards

  • Business registration and incorporation document verification
  • Director and beneficial owner identity verification
  • Proof of regulatory authorisation and licensing
  • Assessment of business activity and source of funds
  • Adverse media and sanctions list screening
  • Periodic KYC review and document refresh

AML Controls — Transaction Monitoring

All transactions are subject to automated monitoring against applicable threshold limits prescribed by RBI guidelines. Transactions that meet or exceed prescribed limits trigger enhanced review protocols.
Suspicious transactions are identified through defined indicators and reported through applicable regulatory reporting channels in accordance with PMLA obligations and RBI guidelines on suspicious transaction reporting.
Partners assessed as higher risk during onboarding are subject to enhanced due diligence procedures and increased transaction monitoring frequency. Risk assessments are documented and reviewed periodically.
Transaction records and KYC documentation are maintained in accordance with the retention periods prescribed by applicable law, in a format that supports regulatory inspection and audit requirements.
Risk Management

Risk Management Philosophy and Practice

A preventive risk management approach — risks identified and addressed at the point of policy design, not managed retrospectively.

Operational Risk

Process-level controls, system redundancy, and defined escalation procedures minimise service disruption risk.

Partner Risk

Structured due diligence, onboarding criteria, and ongoing monitoring govern partner relationships throughout their lifecycle.

Regulatory Risk

Regulatory developments monitored and incorporated into procedures within required timelines.

Fraud Risk

Anti-fraud measures embedded in transaction flows — velocity checks, pattern monitoring, and partner accountability structures.

Technology Risk

High-availability infrastructure with access controls and security configurations appropriate for financial data handling.

Compliance Risk

Internal reviews assess guideline adherence across functions. Findings documented and addressed through corrective action processes.

Data Security & Confidentiality

Data Handling and Security Obligations

Data security as a fundamental operational obligation. Customer data, transaction records, and partner information handled per the IT Act, 2000 and applicable rules.

  • Data is stored on secured systems with access controls restricted to authorised personnel
  • Encryption standards applied to sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Audit logs maintained for access and modification of sensitive records
  • Data sharing with third parties limited to applicable regulatory requirements and partner agreements
  • Incident response procedures documented for data security events
  • Employee access subject to role-based authorisation and confidentiality obligations
Partner-Only Operations Disclaimer Quanture Calanytics Private Limited does not directly collect, store, or process end-consumer personal data except where mandated by applicable regulatory requirements for transaction processing. End-consumer KYC and data obligations rest primarily with the authorised partner institutions conducting direct consumer-facing operations.
