Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 1 January 2025  |  Last Reviewed: March 2025

This Privacy Policy applies to Quanture Calanytics Private Limited ("the Company," "we," "us," or "our") and governs the collection, use, storage, and disclosure of information in connection with the Company's operations and this website. This policy is addressed primarily to institutional partners, business visitors, and other B2B contacts.

1. About Us

Quanture Calanytics Private Limited is a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, engaged in the provision of regulated digital payment and financial infrastructure services in India. The Company operates across B2B and B2C segments, serving institutional partners, business correspondents, and consumers.

2. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy covers:

  • Information collected through this website (quanturecalanytics.com)
  • Information provided by institutional partners, prospective partners, and business contacts
  • Information processed in connection with service delivery to partner institutions

This policy does not govern the data processing activities of the Company's partner institutions in respect of their own end customers. Partners are responsible for their own data protection obligations under applicable law.

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Website Visitors

When you visit this website, we may collect standard technical information including IP address (anonymised where feasible), browser type, pages visited, and referral source through standard web analytics tools. This information is used solely for website administration and improvement purposes.

3.2 Contact and Enquiry Forms

When you submit an enquiry through our website contact form, we collect the information you provide, including name, designation, organisation name, email address, phone number, and the content of your message. This information is used solely to respond to your enquiry.

3.3 Partner Onboarding and Operations

In connection with institutional partner onboarding and service delivery, we collect business information including entity details, director/authorised signatory information, KYC documentation, and transaction-related information as required by applicable regulatory obligations and our internal compliance procedures.

4. How We Use Information

Information collected is used for the following purposes:

  • Responding to enquiries and business communications
  • Partner due diligence, onboarding, and KYC compliance
  • Service delivery and operational management
  • Regulatory reporting and compliance obligations under applicable law
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) monitoring and suspicious activity reporting where required
  • Internal record-keeping, audit, and governance purposes

5. Legal Basis for Processing

The Company processes information on the following bases:

  • Contractual necessity — for fulfilment of partner agreements and service delivery obligations
  • Legal obligation — for compliance with applicable regulatory requirements under RBI guidelines, PMLA, IT Act, and other applicable statutes
  • Legitimate interest — for business operations, partner relationship management, and internal governance
  • Consent — where specifically sought and documented

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

The Company does not sell, rent, or trade personal or institutional information. Information may be disclosed in the following circumstances:

  • To regulatory authorities, law enforcement bodies, or courts as required by applicable law or court order
  • To banking sponsor partners for settlement, KYC verification, or compliance purposes under applicable frameworks
  • To technology vendors and service providers on a need-to-know basis, bound by confidentiality obligations
  • In the context of a business combination, merger, or acquisition, subject to applicable legal requirements

7. Data Retention

The Company retains information for the periods required by applicable law. Transaction records and KYC documentation are retained for a minimum period as prescribed by the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 and applicable RBI guidelines. General business records are retained in accordance with the Companies Act, 2013 and tax legislation.

8. Data Security

The Company implements technical and organisational measures appropriate to the nature of the information held, including access controls, encryption for sensitive data in transit and at rest, audit logging, and employee confidentiality obligations. No technology can guarantee absolute security; however, the Company maintains industry-appropriate standards for financial data handling.

9. Website Cookies

This website may use limited functional cookies necessary for basic website operation. No tracking or marketing cookies are deployed without explicit user consent. Users may configure browser settings to decline cookies, which may affect some website functionality.

10. Third-Party Links

This website may contain links to external websites. The Company is not responsible for the privacy practices or content of linked external sites. Visitors are encouraged to review the privacy policies of any external sites they visit.

11. Rights of Individuals

Individuals who have provided personal information in connection with business enquiries or operations may contact the Company to request access to, correction of, or deletion of such information, subject to applicable legal retention obligations. Requests should be directed to compliance@quanturecalanytics.com.

12. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes in applicable law or the Company's practices. Updated versions will be published on this page with a revised effective date. Continued use of this website or engagement with the Company following publication of an updated policy constitutes acknowledgement of the changes.
